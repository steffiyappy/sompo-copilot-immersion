Tips and prompts

Quick reference for after the session.

Prompt hygiene Common patterns What Copilot will not do

✍ Prompt hygiene for CGA

  • Start with role. "I am the PMO Lead / Head of Operations / underwriter at Contoso General Assurance Indonesia". This alone lifts quality.
  • Name the audience. "steering committee", "regional claims team", "underwriters, 1 to 15 years experience". Copilot calibrates tone accordingly.
  • Say the language. "Bahasa Indonesia formal" or "English, calm and factual". Do not assume.
  • Set the length. "300 words", "one page", "12 bullet points". Vague length equals vague output.
  • Cite the source. "Use the attached file 03_CGA_RAID_Log.xlsx", or "Use publicly available AAJI/AAUI data" if grounding externally.

🔁 Common patterns

  • Turn a doc into a deck. Copilot Chat, "Create a presentation from 02_CGA_SteeringCommitteeBrief.docx".
  • Turn a transcript into a process map. Copilot in Word, ask for an as-is table with owners and hand-offs.
  • Turn a survey into a to-be design. Copilot Chat with the xlsx, ask for bottlenecks plus KPIs plus a to-be process step table.
  • Turn a policy manual into an SOP. Prompt-in-doc inside the manual, request a new SOP grounded on a specific section.
  • Turn a to-be design into a warm change email. Outlook, Draft with Copilot from the Chat B2 output, then Coaching for a final tone check.
What Copilot will not do for CGA. Approve a claim, bind a policy, price a risk, replace an underwriter's judgement, or read data outside your Microsoft 365 tenant unless you connect it. Copilot accelerates the humans; it does not replace them.
